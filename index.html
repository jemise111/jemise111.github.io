About Me

My name is Jesse, and I'm a web developer. I'm a recent graduate of General Assembly's Web Development Immersive program, a 12-week bootcamp that teaches the fundamentals of front-end and back-end development. During my time there, I learned about all aspects of the web application building process, from design to deployment. This includes using RESTful architectures, Agile and Test-Driven methodologies, integrating APIs, and a variety of languages and technologies.

After graduating from Tufts University in 2012, I worked at Balyasny Asset Management, a multi-strategy hedge fund, where I researched equities in the tech and internet sectors and gave investment recommendations. While at Balyasny, I met executives behind some of the most interesting and powerful projects on the web today. The more time I spent focused on the internet sector, the more I realized my interest was in the products, not the financial statements. That is when I decided to transition to the world of web development.
